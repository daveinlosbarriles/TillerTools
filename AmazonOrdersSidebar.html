Request your orders from Amazon

Click this link, login to Amazon.com, and request that they make a zip file of all of your orders available to you. They will send you an email asking for confirmation, and after you confirm, you will receive another email with a link to the zip file. Download this zip file to your computer, and import it here. Note that it may take them a few days to complete the request.

No file selected

After Amazon sends you the ZIP file, use Choose orders ZIP file to select it on your computer and continue.

Expected CSV files:

Order history.csv: Contains every Amazon order you’ve ever made.

Orders returns (actually called Refund details.csv): Contains the dollar amount of every return you’ve made, but no description of what was returned – only an OrderID. If you search by OrderID on your Transactions tab you might be able to figure out what you returned.

Digital orders: Every digital order.

Digital returns: And every digital return. Again, no information on what was returned – just an amount and an order ID.

Whole Foods/Amazon Fresh: Not everyone wants to track and report on individual grocery items from Whole Foods or Amazon Fresh. Uncheck this box if you don’t want to import these individual items.

    NOTE: Duplicate transactions are never imported.

    Allows you to limit how far back you go when importing orders into Tiller. You may not want that book you bought in 1999 in Tiller! We recommend overlapping a month or two from your last import, just to not miss any transactions.

    Note that you can run import multiple times – duplicate transactions will not be created.

    Suggest creating a category “Amazon”, and assign all offsets to that category. Then excluding that category from reporting.

    Also, all of your credit card transactions on Amazon orders should also be set to “Amazon.” This way, they cancel each other out, are both excluded from reporting, and you are only reporting on the specific items you bought, and the categories you assigned to them.

    Note that we have to create offsets when importing your spending, to avoid counting that spending twice – first with your credit card transaction, and then again with the Amazon imported transaction.

    The orders have these payment methods:

    You may have more than one credit card in Amazon. For each type of credit card in Amazon, you need to specify which Tiller account it corresponds to. You can find the account details on the Accounts tab. If you want to manually plug in your own values, leave the various account columns blank on AMZ Import.

    Update any incorrect or Unknown account information on the AMZ Import tab before clicking Import.

    This process may take up to 2 minutes for large Tiller and Amazon files.
